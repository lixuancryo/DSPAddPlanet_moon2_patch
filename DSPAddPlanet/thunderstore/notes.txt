DSPAddPlanet 3.0 notes

Internal plugin version: 3.0.11

Installed theme names:
GiganticForest
RedForest
SulfurSea
MoltenWorld
Beach
Pandora
Obsidian
HotObsidian
Inferno
OilGiant

DwarfPlanet and Comet were removed because very small planet themes are unstable with the vanilla planet generator.

Use them in config.xml like this:
<ThemeName>GiganticForest</ThemeName>

Do not use ThemeId and ThemeName on the same planet. Pick one.

Random galaxy generation switch:
<EnableGalacticScaleThemesInRandomGeneration>false</EnableGalacticScaleThemesInRandomGeneration>

false: GalacticScale-style themes are only used when a planet explicitly has <ThemeName>.
true: the normal galaxy generator may also pick these themes.

Random generation notes:
MoltenWorld uses the same random generation slot as Lava, with about 20% of Lava's weight.
RedForest, Beach, Pandora and GiganticForest can appear in organic positions and cold positions when the random switch is true.

Beach and BeachCold use a GalacticScale-style GS2 vein layout:
Silicium, Bamboo, Fractal and Grat.

3.0.11 note:
SulfurSea ground is now forced to a darker matte sulfur-rock material. This keeps the sulfur ocean and GS-style terrain while avoiding the washed-out land appearance seen with the brighter GalacticScale tint.

For Inferno and OilGiant, set:
<GasGiant>true</GasGiant>

For the other added themes, set:
<GasGiant>false</GasGiant>

These are standalone GalacticScale-style themes for DSPAddPlanet. They reuse the game's own planet algorithms and materials, with GalacticScale-inspired colors, vegetation, gas/resource settings and theme names. They do not enable GalacticScale's full galaxy generator or its custom terrain algorithm system.

DSPAddPlanet still declares GalacticScale 2 as incompatible. If GalacticScale is enabled, DSPAddPlanet may not load.
